We simulate data using 4 markers total \({Y_1, Y_2, Y_3, Y_4}\). The baseline model consists of \({Y_1, Y_2, Y_3}\) while the expanded model includes all markers.
\[ \begin{aligned} X_D & \sim N(\mu_D, \sigma^2) \\ X_{\bar D} & \sim N(0, \sigma^2) \\ \end{aligned} \]

This induces a linear logistic model for \(Risk(X) = Pr(D=1 | X) = expit(\beta X)\).

For discrete and bounded markers, we then round to the nearest integer (for discrete) or truncate at qnorm(.8) = 0.84. This means that all models, besides the expanded continuous models, are mis-specified. I still need to check to make sure that the models are not too severely mis-calibrated.

Markers labeled strong have marginal \(\mu_D = 0.75\) while markers labeled weak have marginal \(\mu_D=0.25\).
